who am i

Dan Gonnella

SRF Scientist at SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory

I am currently an SRF Scientist at SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory as part of the LCLS-II project. My work focuses on cavity and cryomodule testing and acceptance in conjunction with the LCLS-II partner labs - Fermilab and Jefferson Lab. Prior to joining SLAC, I completed my graduate studies in Cornell's superconducting radio frequency group in June 2016. The Cornell SRF group focuses on designing and optimizing niobium SRF cavities to be used in current and future particle accelerators. My work focused on investigating the effects of cavity preparation methods on the superconducting properties of SRF cavities. Specifically I worked to improve the cryogenic efficiency of accelerating structures to make way for new more cost effective colliders and light sources.

My advisor in graduate school was Matthias Liepe and I routinely worked with my fellow graduate students, Sam Posen, Daniel Hall, and James Maniscalco. I had been intimately involved with the LCLS-II project at SLAC during my graduate work prior to joinging the SLAC team, specifically contributing to High Q R&D as part of the LCLS-II High Q collaboration with Cornell, Jefferson Lab, Fermilab, and SLAC.
