who am i

Dan Gonnella

Graduate Student in Accelerator Physics

I am currently a graduate student in Cornell's superconducting radio frequency group. I have been working with the group since Fall 2011. We focus on designing and optimizing niobium SRF cavities to be used in current and future particle accelerators. My work focuses on investigating the effects of cavity preparation methods on the superconducting properties of SRF cavities. Specifically I have been working to improve the cryogenic efficiency of accelerating structures to make way for new more cost effective colliders and light sources.

My advisor in the SRF group is Matthias Liepe and I routinely work with my fellow graduate students, Sam Posen, Daniel Hall, and James Maniscalco. I have been intimately involved with the LCLS-II project at SLAC, specifically contributing to High Q R&D as part of the LCLS-II High Q collaboration with Cornell, Jefferson Lab, Fermilab, and SLAC.
